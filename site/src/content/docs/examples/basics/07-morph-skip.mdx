Some elements are owned by something other than kerf — Chart.js canvases, Monaco editors, third-party widgets. The diff must not recurse into them. `data-morph-skip` is the contract: any element with that attribute (any value, even empty) and its entire subtree are preserved verbatim across re-renders.

**What to look at:** the `<canvas>` is mounted *once* outside of kerf and runs its own `requestAnimationFrame` loop. The surrounding counter re-renders on every click, but the canvas isn't even visited by the diff — its imperative drawing continues uninterrupted.

If you remove `data-morph-skip`, every click re-runs the diff over the canvas — clobbering whatever the imperative `tick()` drew.
